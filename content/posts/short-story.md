There is a ticket booth at the edge of the city that doesn't sell tickets to other cities. It only sells tickets to *away*. 

Elian found it on a Tuesday when the rain was hitting the asphalt like static. He bought a ticket and stepped onto the rusted platform. The train that arrived was a bullet of polished chrome, entirely out of place in the industrial decay. 

He sat down. The seats were velvet. 

"Where exactly does this go?" he asked the conductor, a man with tired eyes and a perfectly pressed uniform.

"It doesn't," the conductor replied, punching a hole in the ticket. "It just leaves."

For the first time in his life, Elian closed his eyes and actually slept.

> *Sometimes the destination isn't a place, but the absence of one.*
